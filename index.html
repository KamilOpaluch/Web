Survival analysis for stock market

Exploring stock market behavior using survival analysis can uncover some fascinating insights into how stocks move and their inherent volatility.
Typically used in medical research to study the time until an event occurs, survival analysis can be adapted to analyze financial events, such as significant price drops or notable returns.

In this experiment, Kaplan-Meier survival curves and Cox Proportional Hazards models are employed to study various stocks.
Kaplan-Meier curves estimate the probability that a stock will avoid a specific event over time, such as a 10% drop in price.
Cox models, on the other hand, examine the impact of various factors, such as trading volume and technical indicators, on the likelihood of these events.
